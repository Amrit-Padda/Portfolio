General FAQ

BitKye is based on the concept of a Rotating Credit and Savings Association (ROSCA). A ROSCA operates on a trust model, where members of a community will come together and contribute to a group fund. After a given amount of time, a member of the ROSCA will withdraw the money from the fund, this will continue as long as the group exists.

Traditionally, ROSCA's have been popular in developing economies and among immigrant communities, where access to formal financial institutions is limited. A ROSCA allows people to have access to capital without many of the barriers to entry.

The rules which govern who can collect the fund depend on the structure of the ROSCA. In some systems, the collector is decided based on 'turns' where each member is guaranteed a collection. In others, the collector is the person who bids the highest amount. This is the model BitKye operates on.

We leverage the Bitcoin Blockchain to process payments and distribute pool funds. Simply transfer the pool buy in amount to the BitKye address and that's it! You're automatically added to the next queue.

BitKye will distribute the funds of a Kye to the highest bidder. The excess amount (any amount greater than the buy-in amount) is distributed to the Kye contributers as interest.
When you decide to join a pool, you will have to enter an amount greater than the pool buy in amount in order to take home the Kye. Any amount over the pool buy in amount is distributed to the other pool members as interest.
When you contribute to a Kye, and are not the highest bidder, you will recieve interest. Any amount greater than the buy-in amount is considered interest, and is distributed evenly to all Kye contributers. For example, if the buy-in amount is 0.011 BTC, and the winner bids 0.031 BTC, the 9 other members will split the 0.02 BTC interest evenly, representing a return of 18%.
